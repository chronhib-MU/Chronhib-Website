Chronologicon Hibernicum (ChronHib) was a research project in the Maynooth University Department of Early Irish, led by Principal Investigator Prof. David Stifter. The project received funding from the European Union's Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme under grant agreement no. 647351. From 2015 ‒ 2021, the ChronHib Team was working on refining the methodology for dating Early Medieval Irish language developments (ca. 6th–mid 10th century A.D.) and on building a chronological framework of linguistic changes that can then be used to date literary texts within the Early Irish period. A major output of the project is the lexicographic database Corpus PalaeoHibernicum (CorPH) that currently includes over 70 deeply annotated Old and Middle Irish texts.
